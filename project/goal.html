Our Project Goal: A Smart DNA-Based Drug Delivery System

One of the biggest hurdles in medicine today is the navigational challenge of modern therapeutics. Conventional drugs distribute systemically, causing off-target effects and significant side effects. Studies show that a very small fraction of administered drugs reach their intended targets, which greatly limits therapeutic efficacy. To address this problem, we aim to create an autonomous nanomachine—a smart Drug Delivery System (DDS)—that releases drugs "only at the right place, at the right time."

Our system consists of two core functional parts:

  • The Vehicle: A DNA hydrogel acting as a biocompatible carrier to encapsulate therapeutic payloads.
  • The Brain: A DNA toolbox that senses the environment and instructs the vehicle when to release the drug.

Goals for BIOMOD

For this year's BIOMOD competition we focus on the foundational steps that demonstrate autonomous control and basic locomotion.

  1. Establish Fundamental Control of a DNA Hydrogel
    Use output signals from the DNA toolbox oscillator to drive rhythmic expansion and contraction of the gel, demonstrating active autonomous control.
  2. Achieve Directed Motion
    Combine gels oscillating out of phase to generate net directional movement, analogous to peristalsis, to show the system can perform mechanical work.

Our Long-Term Vision

This project is the first step toward a long-term roadmap to develop fully autonomous medical nanorobots. Our planned steps are:

  1. Step 1: Establish autonomous control over a DNA hydrogel using the DNA toolbox.
  2. Step 2: Demonstrate basic directional movement (e.g., forward/backward, left/right).
  3. Step 3: Enable more complex continuous behaviors and maneuvers.
  4. Step 4: Integrate biological signals from living systems to guide movement.
  5. Step 5: Achieve a self-sustaining autonomous gel capable of performing tasks in situ.

Conclusion

Our work establishes a versatile and programmable platform for dynamic DNA-based materials, opening a new chapter for smart therapeutics.
